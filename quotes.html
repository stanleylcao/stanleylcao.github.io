Quotes & Reflections

The only true wisdom is in knowing you know nothing.

Socrates
Reflection

There is a paradox at the heart of this: to claim knowledge of one's own ignorance is itself a kind of knowing. But perhaps the point is less about logic and more about posture — the willingness to remain open, to resist the comfort of certainty. It is not that we must know nothing, but that we must never stop being surprised by how much we don't.

不知周之梦为蝴蝶与，蝴蝶之梦为周与。

庄子，《齐物论》
Reflection

Am I a person who dreamed of being a butterfly, or a butterfly now dreaming of being a person? Zhuangzi's question dissolves the boundary between dreamer and dream. It suggests that identity is less fixed than we suppose — that the self we cling to might be one costume among many, worn lightly and shed without grief.

We do not see things as they are, we see them as we are.

Anaïs Nin
Reflection

Perception is a mirror before it is a window. Every landscape we observe, every person we encounter, every book we read — we are meeting ourselves in disguise. This is both a limitation and an invitation: to understand the world better, we might begin by understanding ourselves.
